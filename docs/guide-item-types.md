# PathFinder Guide Item Types

A quick reference for venue operators setting up a non-location guide.

Item type tells the AI what kind of content it is working with. It affects how the AI describes the item to visitors and how it surfaces the item in response to questions. You do not need to use item type — it is optional — but setting it helps the AI answer more naturally.

---

## Physical place

**Use for:** A real location within the venue that has a specific spot visitors can stand in or walk to. Even in a non-location guide, some items may have a physical presence worth naming.

**Examples:**

- The front entrance of a historic house
- The outdoor sculpture garden within a larger campus
- The check-in desk at a food pantry

**When to use instead of Room:** Use "physical place" when it is outdoors or spans a large area. Use "room" for enclosed interior spaces.

---

## Exhibit

**Use for:** A curated display or collection of objects, art, or information that visitors look at or experience in one spot.

**Examples:**

- "19th Century Farm Tools" — a display case at a historic site
- "Local Wildlife Photography" — a rotating wall exhibit at a nature center
- "African Masks Collection" — a gallery section at a small museum

**Tips:** Write the short description from the visitor's perspective — what will they see or feel standing there, not just what the objects are.

---

## Room

**Use for:** A named interior space with its own identity and content. The AI will describe it as a place to enter and explore.

**Examples:**

- The Kitchen — at a historic house
- The Parlor — at a historic house
- The Intake Room — at a service organization
- The Reading Room — at a library or archive

**Tips:** Include in the description what the room was _used for_ historically or currently, not just what it looks like.

---

## Sculpture

**Use for:** An individual artwork that is a freestanding three-dimensional object. Use this instead of "exhibit" when each piece stands alone and visitors encounter them one at a time.

**Examples:**

- Any individual sculpture at Laumeier Sculpture Park
- A public monument or memorial
- A commissioned installation piece

**Tips:** This type is especially useful for sculpture parks where each piece has its own story, artist, and context. The AI will treat it as something to be interpreted, not just located.

---

## Service step

**Use for:** A step in a process that visitors need to follow. Best for organizations where visitors complete a sequence of actions to receive a service.

**Examples:**

- "Step 1: Check In at the Front Desk" — food pantry
- "Step 2: Meet with Your Case Coordinator" — social services
- "Step 3: Collect Your Food Order" — food pantry
- "Medication Pickup Process" — pharmacy or clinic

**Tips:** Number or sequence your service steps using the name field (e.g., "Step 1: …") and describe exactly what the visitor needs to do in plain language. Write for someone who is nervous or unfamiliar with the process.

---

## FAQ

**Use for:** Common questions visitors ask, grouped as a single guide item. The AI will treat this item as a source of answers to frequent questions and draw from it when a visitor asks something that matches.

**Examples:**

- "Common Questions About Tours" — at a historic house
- "What to Know Before You Come" — at any venue
- "Food Pantry Visitor FAQ" — at a service organization
- "Questions About Accessibility" — any venue

**Tips:** Write the long description as a series of actual Q&A pairs in plain text. For example:

> Can I bring my kids? Yes, children are welcome at all times.
> Is parking free? Yes, parking is free in the lot behind the building.
> Do I need an appointment? No appointment is needed for food pickup.

The AI will pull from this content naturally when a visitor asks a matching question.

---

## Amenity

**Use for:** A practical facility or convenience that exists to support visitors. Not a destination in itself, but something visitors will ask about.

**Examples:**

- Restrooms
- Water fountain
- Accessible entrance
- Seating area or rest benches
- Coat check or bag storage
- Vending machines

**Tips:** Keep the description practical and direct. Visitors asking about amenities want quick, clear answers.

---

## Policy

**Use for:** A rule, requirement, or policy that applies to all visitors or a specific part of the experience. The AI will reference this when a visitor asks about what is or is not allowed.

**Examples:**

- "Photography Policy" — museum or gallery
- "Food and Drink Policy" — indoor venue
- "Pet Policy" — outdoor park or venue
- "ID and Documentation Requirements" — social service organization
- "Refund and Cancellation Policy" — ticketed attraction

**Tips:** Write policies in plain, human language. Avoid legal-sounding phrasing. The AI will paraphrase to match the visitor's tone, but clearer source text produces clearer answers.

---

## Activity

**Use for:** Something visitors can actively do, participate in, or experience — not just observe. Distinct from an exhibit (which is viewed) or a service step (which is required).

**Examples:**

- "Self-Guided Audio Tour" — any venue
- "Family Scavenger Hunt" — park or museum
- "Hands-On Craft Station" — children's museum or nature center
- "Guided Birdwatching Walk" — nature center or park
- "Seasonal Apple Picking" — farm or agritourism venue

**Tips:** Include in the description what visitors need to know to participate — any age limits, time requirements, cost, or materials provided.

---

## General info

**Use for:** Content that does not fit any other category. Background information about the venue, its history, its mission, or its context. Also a good default when you are not sure which type to use.

**Examples:**

- "About This Place" — overview of the venue
- "Our History" — founding story of the organization
- "How We Are Funded" — nonprofit context
- "Land Acknowledgment" — cultural or historical context
- "About the Artist" — background on the creator of a work

**Tips:** Use general info for content that gives visitors context for everything else they will experience. The AI will draw from it when visitors ask broad questions like "What is this place?" or "Tell me about the history here."

---

## Quick reference

| Type           | Best for                                             |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Physical place | A named outdoor or large physical area               |
| Exhibit        | A curated display visitors look at                   |
| Room           | A named interior space                               |
| Sculpture      | An individual standalone artwork                     |
| Service step   | A step in a required visitor process                 |
| FAQ            | A collection of common visitor questions             |
| Amenity        | A practical facility (restrooms, parking, etc.)      |
| Policy         | A rule or requirement that applies to visitors       |
| Activity       | Something visitors can actively do or participate in |
| General info   | Background, history, mission, or anything else       |
